About Us

Helping Hands Friends Charitable Trust is a registered NGO in India based primarily in Patna and Prayagraj. We believe that children are the future of our Nation and aim to invest in them today so that tomorrow they bring honor to our country.

Project Aahar

The day hunger disappears, the world will see the greatest spiritual explosion humanity has ever seen. Through Project Aahar HHFC Trust is extending its support to the marginalized by providing food. Our volunteers work hard to provide food to the poor who starve on the road at night. Our objective is to reach all the needy people and provide them with the gift of food. We also distribute the leftover occasion food to the less fortunate once as we believe that Anna-Daan Seva is nectar for the poor. During Covid- 19 we also provided food packets and raw materials among them. Our only aim behind Project Aahar is to extend our helping hands to the underprivileged community to help counter poverty and hunger and establish a healthy community

Rahat Campign

Winters are a tough time. The temperature dropping drastically and the bitterness of winter affects the vast majority of people in unimaginable ways. The Rahat campaign of HHFC Trust is designed to reach out to poor and homeless people and ensure that they are well protected from the harsh winter. We try to reach out to every person in need in areas of Patna, Muzaffarpur, and Chhapra and provide them with warm clothes and blankets. Our volunteers act not less than soldiers to reach every corner of the city.

Project Poshak

Happiness doesn't result from what we receive but from what we donate. HHFC Trust believes that all we need is a true desire to make life better for others. Project Poshak aims to distribute old clothes in different regions of Bihar. The pleasure of fulfilling the basic requirements of people gives warmth to us. The core objective of clothes distribution activities by HHFC Trust is to reduce the suffering of economically and socially backward people. Throughout the year many people visit our organization to donate old clothes to the poor. We as an organization feel about the destitute and we ensure that nobody should suffer the lack of clothes.

project Disha

An Investment In Knowledge Pays The Best Interest! HHFC Trust believes that education is the weapon that cuts down all barriers. Under this project, we spread awareness in slum areas about the importance of education and learning. We make people aware of their living standards. Project Disha aims to support academic, health, and social links between urban and rural areas by meeting the principle of human development goals. Our goal is to connect the under-privileged class to middle and higher-class people. HHFC Trust's main intent is to transform the lives of the slum children through education and provide them with the best. Our program gives support to many children from the slums and helps them overcome all hindrances and attain a quality education and develop their potential to lead a respectful life.

Project Swasthya

Being healthy and fit isn't a fad or a trend, it's a lifestyle. To create a healthy and fit society by popularizing preventive measures, HHFC Trust organizes awareness programs and health camps on various issues. We bring necessary healthcare to the children, women, and poor in slum areas. These sessions and camps are organized in coordination with renowned healthcare institutions under expert professionals. They visit our centers for checkups of the children. Project Swasthya provides patients with free medical consultations. The ongoing dental check-up is a part of our program. Our project encourages preventive health, balanced nutrition, and child and women development in underprivileged settlements. We ensure that the program may reach the maximum number of people so that our mission to build an account table and sustainable health ecosystem is acquired.

Badhte Kadam

The development of the life cycle starts with making a path of education for children. Badhate Kadam is an initiative by HHFC Trust that started in 2021. Under this project, we have taken up the responsibility of enrolling the maximum number of slum children of Patna in schools. We also provide each child with stationery and take up the responsibility for their expenses. A Nation's growth and prosperity depend upon its youth. With this vision, we believe that one day these little stars will brighten up the world with their success stories. This initiative is a noble contribution by HHFC Trust to raise young brilliant minds. To date, we have successfully enrolled 60 slum kids in school. We take pride in shaping these kids for their bright future.

Avsar 2.0

Co curricular activities help us in developing critical abilities and skills to think more logically and work effectively. During pandemic, people were in a devastating phase and they were finding escape from the monotonous situation. So we at HHFC planned an online event platform for kids and adults to showcase their talent. The event consisted of content writings and art and craft. Winners from all the groups were awarded and all the participants were given certificates for their amazing works. We were delighted by their response.

Project Vanita

Sex education is essential to create healthy self-knowledge and reconciliation, healthy conversation, mindsets, and lifestyles. Helping Hands Friends Charitable Trust strongly feels the need to get children and young adults to understand the difference between good touch and bad touch. Therefore we have started counseling and teaching moral values to children and making them understand right and wrong. We also teach them how to tackle these situations if faced through self-defense activities. Through our Project Vanita, we organize preventive programs and sensitively offer appropriate knowledge. Not only this we also create awareness of different aspects of menstruation for girls and women through seminars and workshops. We are working towards breaking the shame and silence about menstruation in rural and slum areas by addressing their menstrual hygiene challenges by establishing menstruation as normal in rural culture. Adding to this project we are planning for livelihood and providing sanitary pads at a portable price to women of all slums.
